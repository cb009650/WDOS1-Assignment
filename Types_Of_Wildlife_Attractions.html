1. Sinharaja Rain forest

One of the very few, but most beautiful rainforests of Sri Lanka. Sinharaja rain forest is a sanctuary to many varieties of plants, birds and animals that take refuge in the moist and murky rain forest.

Hiking through this piece of Eden will leave you truly mesmerized and in awe, you may follow the gentle streams that lead to exhilarating waterfalls, while a cool breeze dances with the trees, visitors may spot all the exotic birds and look out for different types of reptiles and mammals lurking in the forest. With over 130 endemic species living throughout Sinharaja rain forest, visitors have the opportunity to observe as many as they may spot. Therefore be prepared for an unforgettable experience.

The forest has a rich and vibrant history which dates back 1000s of years. The Sinharaja rain forest however has been diluted from its former glory, throughout the years due to colonizers and different industries taking up parts of land. Yet the small piece that remains, still stands as symbol for Sri Lankas beauty and grace. UNESCO declared the rain forest as a World heritage site in 1988 as majority of trees are considered to be rare, further many of the animals in the reservation are endangered and therefore must be protected.

2. Horton Plains

All the way up in the highlands in Sri Lanka, sitting 2100 meters above sea level, Horton Plains is a sanctuary which provides peace and serenity to all who visit. While Horton Plains has only a few more than 20 different species of mammals, there is a large range of birds and flora that visitors may observe.

Considered by locals as one of the coldest and windiest locations in the country, Horton plains is of great importance to the surrounding eco-systems as many rivers begin to spring from there further you may go on an adventure and walk on to see, Worlds End, a cliff with a sheer 4000 foot drop and a famous attractions which draws in many people. Horton plains was discovered by a farmer in the early 1800s and declared as natural reserve in 1969, very recently UNESCO declared it a World Heritage Site on July 2010. As majority of the landscape has not been properly explored and therefore provides an opportunity for many people to research and learn more about the land and nature.

Overall Horton plains will be the perfect destination for those who want to get more in touch with nature while spotting a few endemic plants and animals. It is a land full of breathtaking views, beautiful scenery, amazing animals and wonders that will leave visitors truly mesmerized.

3. Anawilundawa Wetlands

Anawilundawa Wetlands sanctuary is a home to a range of many interesting butterflies and over a 100 different species of birds, both residential and migratory. These exotic birds can easily be spotted in the sanctuary during different times of the year. It is therefore a famous site for bird-watchers and even nature lovers as the flora and fauna in Anawilundawa Wetlands is astonishing.

Originally, around the 12th century an irrigation system was in place which connected a series of large tanks throughout Anawilundawa in order to maintain paddy fields and other vegetations in the area. However over time these irrigation systems broke down and the area is know fed by flood waters. Yet, the tanks have created a natural habitat for a variety of species including birds, fish and reptiles and still continued to irrigate nearby fields.

Horton Plains Some of the most common yet beautiful plants in Horton Plains are Binara, purple-leafed Nillu, Exacum macranthum, Patana grass, Dwarf Bamboo, Aristea ekloni, Maha Meemana fern, Crosypogen and you can easily spot many different types of ferns, orchids and lichens across the Horton Plains There is over 50 different species of birds and some of the most beautiful ones are the Yellow Eared Bulbul, the Mountain Hawk Eagle, bar-winged flycatcher-shrike, Eumiyas Sordida, Dull Blue Flycatcher, Indian blue robin, Sri Lanka White Eye, grey-headed canary flycatcher, Whistling Thrush and much more. Anawilundawa Wetlands There is a range of plants found in Anawilundawa Wetlands including over a 100 woody plant species, 6o herbaceous plant species, more than 50 different types of shrubs and much more. Anawilundawa Wetlands is a famous bird sanctuary therefore many birds can be spotted throughout the year, including all types of Herons and Egrets, pheasant Tailed Jacna, Great Cormorant, purple swamp hen, white ibis and many more rare species of birds. Furthermore there is also a large collection of fish found which is mainly what draws in so many birds What is unique about Anawilundawa Wetlands is that it has 3 different eco systems in such a small location The beach, the interconnected-water tanks and the mangroves. Therefore it creates a cool climate which is very favorable to many birds, resulting in them choosing Anawilundawa Wetlands as a breeding and nesting ground.
